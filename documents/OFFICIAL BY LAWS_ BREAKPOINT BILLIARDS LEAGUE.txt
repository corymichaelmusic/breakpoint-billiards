  
OFFICIAL
 
BYLAWS:
 
BREAKPOINT
 
BILLIARDS
 
LEAGUE
 
1.  LEAGUE  OBJECTIVE  &  AUTHORITY  
The  Breakpoint  Billiards  League  is  a  competitive  cash-prize  league  dedicated  to  fair  
play,
 
sportsmanship,
 
and
 
the
 
growth
 
of
 
the
 
sport.
 
●  League  Operator  (LO):  The  LO  holds  final  authority  over  all  league  matters,  
including
 
rule
 
interpretations,
 
handicap
 
adjustments,
 
and
 
disciplinary
 
actions.
 ●  Amendments:  These  bylaws  may  be  updated  at  the  start  of  any  session  to  
improve
 
league
 
quality.
 
2.  FINANCIALS  &  PRIZE  FUND  
As  a  cash  league,  financial  integrity  is  paramount.  
●  Match  Dues:  Each  player  is  responsible  for  $20  per  week.  Dues  must  be  paid  in  
full
 
prior
 
to
 
the
 
start
 
of
 
the
 
match
 
via
 
[Breakpoint
 
Billiards
 
App/Cash].
 ●  Sanctioning  Dues:  A  sanctioning  fee  of  $25  per  session  is  required  by  all  
players.
 ●  The  Prize  Fund:  90%  of  the  weekly  match  fees  will  be  paid  out  at  the  end  of  the  
session.
 
Each
 
session’s
 
pay
 
out
 
will
 
be
 
different
 
due
 
to
 
amount
 
of
 
players.
 
1st
 
place
 
will
 
never
 
exceed
 
20%,
 
and
 
last
 
place
 
will
 
never
 
receive
 
less
 
than
 
$100.
 
 ●  Forfeits:  Players  that  forfeit  a  match  are  still  responsible  for  their  weekly  dues.  
Failure
 
to
 
pay
 
may
 
result
 
in
 
removal
 
from
 
the
 
league
 
and
 
loss
 
of
 
eligibility
 
for
 
the
 
end-of-session
 
payout.
 ●  Rescheduling:  Players  that  cannot  attend  league  night,  must  communicate  
through
 
the
 
app
 
to
 
attempt
 
to
 
reschedule
 
with
 
their
 
opponent
 
for
 
that
 
week.
 
If
 
there
 
is
 
no
 
communication
 
through
 
the
 
app,
 
the
 
league
 
operator
 
may
 
assume
 
a
 
forfeit
 
by
 
the
 
absent
 
player(s).
 
Players
 
are
 
allowed
 
up
 
to
 
1
 
week
 
past
 
the
 
date
 
of
 
the
 
match
 
to
 
make
 
it
 
up.
 
If
 
the
 
match
 
is
 
not
 
made
 
up,
 
the
 
league
 
operator
 
has
 
the
 
authority
 
to
 
forfeit
 
1
 
or
 
both
 
players
 
or
 
still
 
allow
 
the
 
match
 
to
 
be
 
made
 
up.
 
If
 
a
 
player
 
reschedules
 
more
 
than
 
2
 
matches
 
in
 
a
 
session,
 
it
 
is
 
at
 
the
 
league
 
operators
 
discretion
 
to
 
take
 
disciplinary
 
action.
  
 

3.  HANDICAPPING  &  RATINGS  
The  "Breakpoint  Billiards  Rating  System  (BBRS)"  is  designed  to  allow  players  of  all  skill  
levels
 
to
 
compete
 
fairly.
 
●  Handicap  Scale:  Players  are  rated  on  a  scale  of  0.0  to  10.0.  ●  Race  Matrix:  Breakpoint  Billiards  has  established  a  race  matrix  that  is  fair  for  
both
 
players.
 
This
 
matrix
 
is
 
not
 
public
 
because
 
it
 
is
 
proprietary
 
information.
 
 ●  Initial  Rating:  New  players  without  an  established  history  will  be  assigned  a  
"Starting
 
Rating"
 
by
 
the
 
LO.
 
For
 
at
 
least
 
the
 
first
 
year
 
of
 
Breakpoint
 
Billiards
 
operation,
 
this
 
rating
 
will
 
be
 
based
 
on
 
an
 
established
 
Fargo
 
rating.
 
If
 
a
 
player
 
doesn’t
 
have
 
an
 
established
 
fargo
 
rating,
 
The
 
player
 
must
 
have
 
an
 
initial
 
rating
 
of
 
“5.0”.
 
 ●  Sandbagging:  Any  player  found  to  be  intentionally  playing  below  their  skill  level  
to
 
maintain
 
a
 
lower
 
handicap
 
will
 
be
 
subject
 
to
 
review
 
and
 
potential
 
disqualification.
 
4.  ADDING  PLAYERS  
●  Adding  players:  The  Breakpoint  Billiards  system  does  not  allow  for  players  to  be  
added
 
after
 
the
 
schedule
 
has
 
been
 
made.
 
 
5.  MATCH  PLAY  &  EQUIPMENT  
●  Rules  of  Play:  Breakpoint  Billiards  uses  BIS  (Breakpoint  International  Standard)  
rules.
 
 ●  Scoring:  Scores  must  be  kept  via  the  Breakpoint  Billiards  App.  Both  players  
must
 
"agree"
 
on
 
the
 
digital
 
scoresheet
 
at
 
the
 
conclusion
 
of
 
the
 
match.
 
Any
 
disputes
 
are
 
handled
 
by
 
the
 
LO.
 
In
 
the
 
event
 
that
 
the
 
app
 
is
 
not
 
functioning,
 
scores
 
must
 
be
 
kept
 
on
 
paper,
 
signed
 
and
 
turned
 
in
 
to
 
the
 
LO.
 ●  Shot  Clock:  While  no  official  shot  clock  is  used,  "Slow  Play"  is  discouraged.  If  a  
match
 
is
 
lagging,
 
the
 
LO
 
may
 
implement
 
a
 
45-second
 
shot
 
clock.
 ●  Start  of  The  Match:  Players  must  flip  a  coin  or  lag.  If  the  players  can’t  agree,  the  
players
 
must
 
flip
 
a
 
coin.
 
The
 
winner
 
of
 
the
 
flip
 
or
 
lag
 
has
 
the
 
option
 
to
 
choose
 
the
 
set
 
that
 
is
 
played
 
first
 
or
 
choose
 
to
 
break
 
first.
 
The
 
player
 
that
 
does
 
not
 
break
 
first
 
in
 
the
 
first
 
set,
 
will
 
break
 
first
 
in
 
the
 
second
 
set.
 
 
Example:
 
Player
 
A
 
wins
 
the
 
flip.
 
Player
 
A
 
chooses
 
to
 
break
 
first.
 
Player
 
B
 
Chooses
 
8
 
ball.
 
Player
 
A
 
breaks
 
in
 
8-ball
 
and
 
Player
 
B
 
breaks
 
in
 
9-ball.
 
 
6.  SPORTSMANSHIP  &  CONDUCT  
●  Etiquette:  Players  are  expected  to  remain  quiet  while  their  opponent  is  at  the  
table.
 
"Sharking"
 
(distracting
 
the
 
opponent)
 
is
 
strictly
 
prohibited.
 ●  Fouls:  If  a  foul  occurs,  players  should  call  it  immediately.  If  a  dispute  arises,  play  
must
 
stop
 
until
 
a
 
LO
 
or
 
a
 
mutually
 
agreed-upon
 
third
 
party
 
can
 
make
 
a
 
ruling.
 ●  Expulsion:  Physical  violence,  verbal  abuse  of  opponents  or  staff,  or  damage  to  
venue
 
property
 
will
 
result
 
in
 
an
 
immediate
 
and
 
permanent
 
ban
 
from
 
Breakpoint
 
Billiards
 
with
 
no
 
refund
 
of
 
dues.

 ●  Session  Payouts:  Session  places  are  determined  by  set  win%.  In  the  event  of  a

rack
 
win%.
 
All

as

as

can

in

Breakpoint
 
Billiards
 
app.
 
Prize
 
money

the
 
end
 
of
 
session
