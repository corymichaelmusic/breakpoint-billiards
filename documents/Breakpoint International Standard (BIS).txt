 
Breakpoint  International  Standard  (BIS)  
Official
 
League
 
Rulebook
 
1.  General  Principles  
The  Breakpoint  International  Standard  (BIS)  is  designed  to  balance  professional,  competitive  
integrity
 
with
 
the
 
practical
 
needs
 
of
 
league
 
play.
 
These
 
rules
 
prioritize
 
sportsmanship
 
and
 
clear
 
communication.
 
 
2.  General  Rules  of  Play  
2.1  The  Coin  Flip  or  Lag  for  Break  
The  coin  flip  is  the  preferred  method  of  deciding  who  breaks  first,  but  players  have  the  option  to  
agree
 
to
 
lag
 
as
 
well.
 
Players
 
shoot
 
simultaneously
 
from
 
the
 
head
 
string
 
to
 
the
 
foot
 
cushion
 
and
 
back.
 
The
 
player
 
whose
 
ball
 
stops
 
closest
 
to
 
the
 
head
 
cushion
 
wins
 
the
 
lag.
 
●  The  winner  may  choose  to  break  or  pass  the  break  to  the  opponent.  ●  In  subsequent  games,  Alternate  break  applies.  ●  In  subsequent  sets,  Alternate  break  applies  to  the  set.   
2.2  Definitions  of  Fouls  (Ball-in-Hand)  
When  a  foul  is  committed,  the  incoming  player  receives  ball-in-hand  anywhere  on  the  entire  
table
.
 
●  Cue  Ball  Scratch:  Pocketing  the  cue  ball  or  driving  it  off  the  table.  ●  Contact:  The  cue  ball  must  contact  a  legal  object  ball  before  contacting  another  ball.  ●  No  Rail:  After  the  cue  ball  contacts  a  legal  object  ball,  at  least  one  ball  (cue  or  any  
object
 
ball)
 
must
 
contact
 
a
 
rail
 
or
 
be
 
pocketed.
 ●  Double  Hit:  Striking  the  cue  ball  twice  with  the  cue  tip  or  pushing  the  cue  ball  when  it  is  
in
 
near-contact
 
with
 
the
 
object
 
ball.
 ●  Illegal  Coaching:  Receiving  tactical  advice  from  teammates  or  spectators  during  a  turn.  
2.3  The  "Limited  Foul"  Exception  (No  All-Ball  Fouls)  

To  prevent  disputes  without  referees,  BIS  utilizes  a  Limited  Foul  system:  
●  Accidental  Movement:  If  a  player  accidentally  moves  a  stationary  ball  (by  hand,  cue  
shaft,
 
or
 
clothing),
 
it
 
is
 
not
 
a
 
foul.
 ●  Restoration:  The  opponent  has  the  sole  right  to  leave  the  ball  where  it  moved  or  restore  
it
 
to
 
its
 
original
 
position.
 
If
 
the
 
players
 
don’t
 
agree
 
where
 
the
 
ball
 
should
 
be
 
placed,
 
opponent’s
 
placement
 
of
 
ball
 
prevails.
 ●  Exception:  If  a  moved  ball  interferes  with  the  path  of  the  cue  ball  during  a  shot,  it  is  a  
foul.
 ●  Break  Fouls:  During  a  break,  4  balls  must  hit  a  rail  or  a  ball  must  be  made.  If  neither  of  
these
 
conditions
 
are
 
met,
 
the
 
break
 
is
 
deemed
 
illegal.
 
In
 
the
 
event
 
of
 
an
 
illegal
 
break,
 
the
 
opponent
 
has
 
the
 
option
 
to
 
play
 
the
 
rack
 
as
 
it
 
is,
 
or
 
to
 
re-rack
 
and
 
break.
 
2.4  Loss  of  Game  
●  Picking  up  the  Cue  Ball  to  Forfeit  a  Shot:  If  a  player  picks  up  the  cue  ball  from  the  
table
 
in
 
order
 
to
 
forfeit
 
a
 
shot,
 
that
 
player
 
forfeits
 
that
 
rack.
 
 
  
 
3.  8-Ball  Rules  
3.1  The  Rack  
A  standard  triangle  rack  with  the  8-ball  in  the  center,  and  head  ball  on  the  spot.  The  two  lower  
corners
 
must
 
contain
 
one
 
stripe
 
and
 
one
 
solid.
 
3.2  Open  Table  
The  table  is  always  Open  immediately  following  the  break.  Even  if  balls  are  pocketed  on  the  
break,
 
the
 
shooter
 
must
 
successfully
 
pocket
 
a
 
"called"
 
ball
 
to
 
claim
 
a
 
group
 
(Stripes
 
or
 
Solids).
 
3.3  Calling  the  Shot  
BIS  requires  Call-Pocket  only.  
●  The  shooter  must  designate  the  ball  and  the  intended  pocket.  ●  Details  such  as  rails,  kisses,  or  caroms  do  not  need  to  be  specified.  ●  Safety:  A  player  may  call  "Safety"  before  a  shot.  Their  turn  ends  after  the  shot,  
regardless
 
of
 
whether
 
a
 
ball
 
is
 
pocketed.
 
3.4  Loss  of  Game  
A  player  loses  the  game  if  they:  
●  Pocket  the  8-ball  and  foul  on  the  same  shot.  ●  Pocket  the  8-ball  in  a  pocket  other  than  the  one  designated.  ●  Pocket  the  8-ball  before  all  of  their  balls  are  pocketed.  ●  Pocket  the  8-ball  and  the  cue  ball  on  the  break.  ●  Knock  the  8-ball  off  the  table.  
3.5  Declaring  the  Winner  of  a  Game  
A  player  wins  the  game  if  they:  
●  Pocket  all  of  their  balls  (solids  or  stripes)  and  correctly  call  and  pocket  the  8  ball.   
 
4.  9-Ball  Rules  
4.1  The  Rack  
Balls  1-9  are  racked  in  a  diamond.  The  1-ball  is  at  the  apex,  and  the  9-ball  is  in  the  center.  
4.2  The  Push-Out  
On  the  shot  immediately  following  a  legal  break,  the  shooter  may  call  "Push-Out."  
●  The  "No  Rail"  and  "Contact"  rules  are  suspended  for  this  shot.  ●  After  the  push-out,  the  opponent  may  choose  to  play  the  shot  as  it  lies  or  pass  the  shot  
back
 
to
 
the
 
shooter.
 
4.3  Three-Foul  Rule  
(will
 
be
 
implemented
 
after
 
inaugural
 
session)
 
If  a  player  commits  a  foul  on  three  consecutive  turns  without  making  a  legal  shot  in  between,  
they
 
lose
 
the
 
game.
 
●  Mandatory  Warning:  The  opponent  must  warn  the  shooter  when  they  are  on  "two  
fouls."
 
If
 
no
 
warning
 
is
 
given,
 
the
 
shooter
 
remains
 
on
 
two
 
fouls.
 
 
5.  Sportsmanship  &  Integrity  
5.1  Close  Hits  
If  a  shot  is  likely  to  result  in  a  "split  hit"  (hitting  the  legal  ball  and  an  illegal  ball  simultaneously),  
the
 
shooter
 
must
 
ask
 
the
 
opponent
 
or
 
a
 
neutral
 
third
 
party
 
to
 
observe
 
the
 
hit.
 
5.2  Benefit  of  the  Doubt  
In  any  situation  where  a  foul  is  contested  and  no  third  party  was  watching,  the  shooter's  
version
 
of
 
events
 
prevails.
